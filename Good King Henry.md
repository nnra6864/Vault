Good King Henry(Chenopodium bonus-henricus) is a perennial vegetable.
It's very low maintenance, highly nutritious, and comes back for 5-10+ years.

## Growing

Good King Henry can be planted in Aprlil-May, just after the last frost, or September-October.
The seeds shouldn't be buried too deep, pressing them into the soil and putting a bit of dirt on top should be enough.
Soil should be moist, but not too wet.
Early-mid spring, it shoots up thick stalks that taste like asparagus.
Around May, it produces leaves that taste like spinach.
This goes on till the frost.
Multiplying this plant is rather easy, either by digging up half the plant in early spring or autumn and immediately planting it elsewhere, or waiting for late spring-summer to obtain some seeds. Keep in mind that it may also spread on its own.

## Usage

Everything above the ground is edible.
### Young Leaves

Can be used just like spinach, either fresh or cooked.
They have a mild flavor.
Best in spring and early summer.

### Older Leaves

Similar to young leaves, but much tougher.
Usually eaten cooked for 2-5 minutes.

### Shoots

Eaten similar to asparagus, usually boiled for 5-10 minutes.
Best harvested when 10-15 cm tall.

### Flower Buds

Can be cooked like broccoli.

## Nutrition

Good King Henry is highly nutritious.
It's a great source of iron, calcium, and vitamins A, B and C.
Most of the vitamin A, C, calcium and iron are found in leaves, while shoots contain more carbohydrates and fibers.
