Stevia can last for years if handled properly.
It likes heat and moderate moisture, not soggy nor bone dry.
Stevia is a distant cousin of the [[Sunflower]].

## Growing

Stevia is native to [[Paraguay]]/[[Brazil]], so it loves the heat and hates the frost.
Plant it outside, preferably in a pot, in [[May]], and bring it inside or harvest all of it by [[October]].
It needs full sun and well drained soil, but it doesn't like to be bone dry.
If roots sit in water, they will rot.

## Harvesting

Stevia is the sweetest and most medicinal right before the flowers grow.
Once the plant starts putting energy into its little white flowers, the sweetness and medicinal properties in the leaves start to drop.
Harvesting method is similar to [[Mint]] and [[Basil]].
Cut the top branches with sharp scissors, just above a set of two leaves, that will make it branch and be bushy.
Big harvest can be made in late [[September]]/[[October]], right before the first frost, cutting the entire plant down, and only leaving ~10cm of the stem above the soil.

## Replanting

Stevia can be replanted similar to [[Mint]].
Whilst it won't spread as fast due to low germination rate, you can speed it up significantly.
Cut the healthy upper 10cm of the stem that has no flowers, strip the leaves off the bottom half to prevent rot, and just dip it in water or moist soil.
Keep it in a humid, warm spot for 2-3 weeks, and it'll grow its own roots.

## Usage

When drying, make sure not to use sun as UV rays will destroy the sweet [[stevioside]]s over time.

### Dried Powder

Powder is the easiest to make, but since the leaves have [[fiber]]/[[chlorophyll]], they won't dissolve and you'll be left with a bit of green [[sediment]] after usage.
It has a very earthy taste with a bit of grassy flavor, like a sweet herbal tea with a strong [[licorice]] finish.

1. Dry the leaves by hanging them or using a low temp oven.
2. Crush them into a green powder with something like a coffee grinder.

Powder/dried leaves will stay good for ~12-18 months, but they won't go bad, they'll just lose their sweetness.

### Liquid Extract

Extract is good due to having a much milder earthy taste, making it taste closer to store bought stuff.
It can be made from both fresh and dried leaves, but dried are preferred due to cell walls being broken down, making it easier for [[alcohol]] to extract the sweet compounds.

1. Put preferably dried leaves in a jar.
2. Cover them with vodka or any other high proof clear alcohol.
3. Let it sit for 24-36 hours, but don't go any longer as it'll become bitter.
4. Strain the leaves out
5. Simmer the liquid on low heat for ~20 minutes to cook off the alcohol.

If stored in the fridge, or a dark cold place, the extract will be good for ~3-6 months.

## Medicinal Properties

### [[Hypoglycemic]]

Some studies suggest that not only does Stevia not raise the blood sugar, it also helps improve [[insulin sensitivity]].
It contains compounds such as stevioside, that help lower blood [[glucose]] levels and improve how your body handles [[insulin]].

### [[Hypotensive]]

In higher doses, Stevia has been shown to act as a mild [[vasodilator]], widening blood vessels, which can help lower the blood pressure.
This is usually observed with long term use of the whole leaf extract.

### [[Anti-cariogenic]]

Unlike sugar, which is fermentable, Stevia is non-fermentable.
That means bacteria can't eat it and produce acid.
This prevents the growth of [[Streptococcus mutans]], the primary cause of cavities.

### [[Anti-microbial]] / [[Anti-bacterial]]

Stevia has the ability to inhibit the growth of certain harmful bacteria and fungi.
Some people use it as a [[topical]] wash for skin irritations like [[seborrhea]] or [[dermatitis]].

### [[Antioxidant]]

Leaves are rich in [[phenols]] and [[flavonoids]], which neutralize free radicals in the body, helping to prevent cellular damage (oxidative stress).

### [[Anti-inflammatory]]

It helps regulate the pathways that cause inflammation in the body.
