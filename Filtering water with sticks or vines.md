Filtering water can easily be done by using freshly cut vines or sticks.
It's important that the sticks are not dried up, and if the edge does dry up, it can simply be cut off to reach a fresh edge.
Stick the bottom side of the stick/vine into the container with contaminated water, and the top side wherever you want the water to go.
This will get rid of most, if not all, bacteria found in water.
To get rid of the viruses and similar, make sure to leave the filtered water in direct sunlight, preferably on a smooth stone to increase the temperature and sun reflection, for at least 6 hours, but possibly up to 12.
You should be left with clean, drinkable water.
It's important to remember that this doesn't get rid of unwanted chemicals found in tap water, or if there's a factory upstream etc.
